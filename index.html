Project Ideas

Overview

The aim of the project will be to develop a smartphone application for the care, treatment and tracking of indoor house plants. Through this app, users will be able to see their collection of plants in list or grid format, see how they should care for their plants, what their plants require, diseases that may affect their plants and how to treat them. Furthermore, users will be able to add a way of tracking water, feeding, and repotting periods and set gentle reminders.

Motivation

The motivation behind this application is personal. During the pandemic, my wife and I found collecting indoor plants as a new hobby as well as a way to decorate our flat. It was however difficult to find good advice on how to care for these specific plants as the small tag provided with the plant was vague and doesn’t outline proper care instructions. This resulted in searching the internet and Facebook groups to seek help on common care requirements, information about diseases and treatments. As there were a large number of social media groups that discussed this topic, I could see the potential for an application that stores all this information in one location to help users care for their plants.

Description

Personalized accounts In order to use the app, users must create an account with a username and password. This account will generate their profile where they will be able to store their plant listing against their account, share with others and log into other devices where their database will already be loaded.

Plant Database Users will be able to search through a database of various indoor plants. From here they will be able to add the plants they have to their ‘owned’ section OR favourite the plants they aspire to eventually purchase. Selecting a plant in this screen will also give the user an overview of the general requirements for owning that plant. For example, light level and positioning, humidity, growth size, etc.

Storing Owned Plants An area in the application where users will be able to see the plants, they currently own. This again will give a general overview of the plants requirements as well as common issues, plant diseases, and treatments.

Tracking An option to track the last time a specific plant was watered, given fertiliser or feed and even track when it’s due to be repotted or given fresh soil.

Common Issues, Pests, and Diseases A main feature of the app will be a page dedicated to listing common issues, pests, and diseases among plant varieties along with treatments and advise. This will include images of the pest or disease on various types of plants, what to look for, and the treatments.

AI Identifier This feature would allow users to open the in the app camera, scan a plant they have and reveal suggestions of what type of plant it is based on a database. It could also feature a quick glimpse at what the requirements are for that plant in doors of light positioning, humidity level, growth size and difficulty. This is an incredibly advanced feature to implement into an app and the time constraint may not allow for this to be added right away.

Tools & technologies

In order for users to be able to create an account, there may be a server or SQL database linked with the application, so users’ information is able to be stored. This might be usernames, passwords and even save the selections they make on their profiles. A database may also need to be used to store relevant information about the various types of plants and diseases. Platforms from the adobe suit like photoshop, illustrator and XD may be required to build mock-ups of landing pages, or edit images. Finally, both VScode and Atom can be used to write the code for the smartphone application.

Skills Required

I think that most of the coding skills required for this program could be accessible online. Although this will be challenging as a novice to coding, there are many websites online with databases dedicated to breaking down specific parts of HTML and CSS coding. A server and/or database will be another crucial element to the application. In order to store users’ data or information about various types of plants, these may need to be implemented. These are many open-source database, management software available online such as MySQL, PostgreSQL, and SQLite.

Outcome

The successful development of this application will result in a good quality database that give users accurate information about caring for their house plants. This database will solve the issue of users having to research various platforms in order to find the answers they are looking for. This will mean less time wasted for users when researching as well as avoid confusion on differing and conflicting answers. As a result, the impact of this smartphone application will help users better understand how to care for their house plants.
